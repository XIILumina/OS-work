## Ability System

### 1) Core Nature: The Cube

- The cube is not a device — it is a carbon-based alien organism, ancient beyond practical measurement
- It spent millions of years dormant underground; excavated and brought to a private lab as an unidentified compound
- On contact with unprotected skin it does not infect — it rewrites; every cell in the host body is converted to carbon-based biology, oxygen-independent, structurally indestructible
- The converted cells retain full versatility: they can restructure their carbon bonds to form bone, marrow, flesh, or other biological structures on demand
- Lumina is not bonded to the cube — she is the cube; the merge was total
- Humanoid body is a materialised carbon shell; she can alter its structure within the rules of the system
- Regenerates from impossible damage as long as the cube core (bioreactor) holds
- Stores consumed matter and consciousness imprint data from absorbed living beings

### 2) Non-Biological Matter Control (High Precision)

- Can reshape non-biomatter materials on contact
- Can convert available material into tools, weapons, barriers, and structures(most useless her ability)
- Can perform high-speed structural reconfiguration during combat
- Practical limit: must source mass/energy from environment or reserves

### 3) Biological Manipulation (Catastrophic)

- Cannot safely sculpt biological matter
- Attempts produce autonomous hostile flesh constructs
- Constructs are fast, predatory, and difficult to contain
- Movement rule: they reposition when not being directly observed
- Lumina does not fully control these constructs after creation

### 4) Biomass Consumption & The Gluttony Curse

- Can absorb biological mass as fuel/reserve
- Consciousness echoes become part of persistent internal psychic noise
- The cube demands constant resupply — not as a physical need but as a psychological siege

**Deprivation Stages (approximate, not rigid):**

| Stage | Timeframe | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| 1 — Itch | Days 1–3 | Heightened irritability, slightly shorter patience, intrusive thoughts about feeding |
| 2 — Pressure | Days 4–7 | Voices from consumed souls grow louder and more coherent, head pain intensifies, sleep-like states become impossible |
| 3 — Craving | Week 2+ | Involuntary partial transformations during idle moments, difficulty maintaining selective restraint, predatory attention sharpens toward nearby targets regardless of guilt |
| 4 — Override | Prolonged | She will consume something. Not because she lost control — because she decides the torment is no longer worth resisting. That choice is the moral weight. |

**Why It Matters (Not a Weakness — A Moral Trap):**
- Her body never fails, never slows, never breaks from hunger
- This makes the curse entirely psychological and ethical
- She can hold out as long as she wants — but the longer she holds, the more she suffers
- The eventual act is inevitable, and she knows it; the only question is who or what she chooses as the target when that moment comes

## Origin Event: The Lab

**Classification:** Contained, unwitnessed, unresolved

**The discovery:**
An excavation team unearthed a dense, irregular mass of unknown material at significant depth. It did not match any catalogued compound. It was brought to a small private research lab for material analysis. The lab was unremarkable — small staff, private funding, no institutional oversight worth noting.

**First contact:**
First physical contact occurred without protective equipment. The result was immediate and irreversible: skin-to-cube contact triggered a full cellular rewrite beginning at the point of touch. The process is not painful in any conventional sense — witnesses described it as a pressure, then a warmth, then nothing, because by then they were already becoming something else.

**Lumina as Patient Zero:**
She was the first. The cube did not choose her in any mystical sense — she was simply the one whose hand reached it first. Her arms went first: flesh dissolved off the bone cleanly, leaving exposed marrow and skeletal structure still functional. The chain reaction spread upward through her body in a resonance cascade .

**The lab:**
The outbreak was unnoticed externally because the lab was too small to warrant monitoring. By the time the cellular rewrite had finished propagating to through the area, most of the staff were either dead or mid-conversion. Lumina — still coherent, still herself in the parts that mattered — killed the rest. From something closer to instinct and the terror of watching what was happening and not being able to stop it.

**The marauders:**
Word got out through some channel — someone who left before the worst of it. Investors and opportunists arrived wanting to extract the cube, monetise it. They found Lumina alone in the building, standing over what was left. She killed them immediately and without ceremony. She did not feel strongly about it at the time.

**The infinite loop:**
The lab bodies do not stay dead. The cube, having already rewritten their biology at the cellular level, refuses to release the claim. They die — and reconstitute. Die again — reconstitute again. Not as abominations, not as mindless hunters. Just as bodies, cycling. The process appears automatic, unconscious, something the cube does the way a living organism breathes. Whether they experience anything in the loop is unknown. Lumina does not talk about this. She witnessed it.
**Current status of the lab:**
Sealed. Location known only to Lumina. She has not returned. This is, depending on how you read her, either trauma avoidance or practical quarantine management. Possibly both.
